In the first week, I was introduced to the web foundations course, being given a rundown on what our specification was for the year and a small insight of the type of things we would begin to learn. Our lecturer divided our total coursework into 2 sections. The first was a 1000 word limit literature review using APAv6 Referencing and Citations. The next coursework was to make a website from scratch combining both HTML5 and CSS3.

In the second week, We were introduced to different editors on a worksheet, The first task was to create a time plan for a week which in my own time I developed into a yearly timetable, not just for web foundations but also for my other subject areas, in order to fulfill the yearly advisory hours requirement. We also picked an editor for our HTML5 coursework, I found that Atom was the best for me as I could add packages and shortcuts which made programming more enjoyable and productive.

In the third week, I was introduced to the inside of the web and the security of the web, the head of computing, Dr. Nick Savage, presented this interesting lecture in which we were shown the ways in which we are vulnerable to data leakage which later I found very helpful when I begin to write my Literature Review. I learned that having a simple password or even a common password just makes being hacked even easier. Even the post we receive is a vulnerability.

In the fourth week, we were introduced in depth with our first coursework which was the literature review. The main topics were ‘mitigating corporate information exposure on the web’. I learned that the university library has a keyword search and so does google scholar which would help me find academic journals in the topic area, Rather than using secondary or uncredited information. Our lecturers guided to these tools to begin our research and make a plan for our 1000 word essay.

In the fifth week, we were given a lecture and practical on Academic Writing, which showed us how we can reference and cite our work in a professional manner using the required APAv6 referencing method. I found this very helpful as from this and the previous week I found that I now have the tools I need to achieve accredited and concise information regarding my topic and have a template for citations and referencing which I am doing as I go along with my literature review.

In the sixth week, we were introduced to Markup languages & HTML for the first time, In this week we were given the science behind or even the history of HTML and markup languages, we let our lecturers know if we have any previous HTML experience and for those like myself who have never used HTML, we were introduced to the absolute background of HTML and markup languages and why we use it on the web. I acknowledged that HTML has been made to make the coding side of things on the webs, the design side was CSS3.

In the seventh week, it was a reading week so we had no lectures and practicals, however this was also the week in which our Literature Review, the first coursework was due, this was our first submission so we had a chance to improve further of the feedback given by our lecturers, this was a good opportunity for me to proofread all my work. One thing I made sure of was having no plagiarism, Everything I wrote myself and researched myself. Personally, I really enjoyed reading on an Author Imad M. Ibadi as his views on the exposure included a solution which was the answer to the question suggesting everything is tracked and recorded in a log which should only be handled by higher authority in the business to track and observe everything going on in the business and providing essential training where necessary to prevent all data exposure on the web.

In the eighth week, we solely began to focus on pure HTML and CSS and this is all that's left, we learned that HTML is only used for content and provides all the information such as the text and the headings etc. CSS was now introduced to us and we found that CSS is actually what we will be using and is commonly used when creating the Form or Design in websites which gives us that appealing look. I looked at an example of a newspaper web page such as BBC, which gave me a clear insight when I used Google Chrome, I right clicked and pressed inspect element, it showed me clearly where HTML was used and where CSS was used, and you can clearly differentiate the two. I found all the HTML was all the text and important information on the website and the rest of it was the design which was in CSS.

In the ninth week, Our lecturers directed us to the best editor in their preference, It was Atom which is what I was already using. They explained the benefit of it over other editors and also told us a few packages, which I myself didn't know about that could make our programming easier, they also mentioned some shortcuts such as tab when u right the beginning of a function in HTML. I tried it when beginning my coding practice for HTML5 and CSS3. I saw that when I wrote HTML and pressed tab it would give me all the base code I needed to write HTML5 code which is extremely helpful. I also learned to use WinSCP to connect my website to an IP address using my virtual machine and display what I've coded in Atom.

In the tenth week, we began to focus much more on CSS. looking at what design techniques we can use when making our own websites for our second and final coursework. In our practical session we used a bunch of words and categorized them in sections for a hotel online website, the idea was to categorize certain topics in a subheading on the website. From this I learned that even over the smallest options my group and I differed in thoughts, so you have to look at the audience when making your website rather than your preference and getting constant feedback on every change is essential and key.

In the eleventh week, we had a whole lecture and practical on using images and the different ways we could do so. Our lecturer showed us all the possible things you need to look at, position of the image, tags when you hover above the image, resizing the image, fixing image size if it comes out wrong and also my personal favourite, changing the image to a smaller each time you have the page reach a certain size in measurement of pixels. In my practical, I asked Dr. Rich Boakes how to adjust the image size if the screen size was reduced. He taught me in CSS where all such editing occurs if I, under the img tag set the max-width; 100%. It would change the size of the screen if it got smaller and if I made the screen bigger it would only get as big as its original size and not bigger to prevent it becoming pixelated.

In the twelfth and final lecture of the first semester, we had more of a fun and interactive lecture without lecturers in which we made an advent calendar for Christmas using images, animations and sounds. The idea of this lecture was to have a little bit of fun with HTML5 and CSS3 but also introduce the sort of things we would be doing in the second year if we chose web-script programming. I find HTML and CSS very interesting and one of the more enjoyable and hands-on practical subjects to do so I definitely am considering it as a high priority for my second year.

In the thirteenth week and the first week of the second semester, we were introduced officially with the second course in depth with all the things we can and cannot use. The Peer Review Marking Guide was also released which shows us what we have to do in order to achieve maximum marks. In this week I began my work on my website and have completed the heading and categories to other pages in which ie also formatted my literature review into it so that it looks clean and professional. I have chosen to be simplistic with my design as instructed by my Lecturers and keep proof-reading my lecture notes to make sure I use only HTML5 and CSS3 and nothing older and also avoid certain tags such as font and br (break). I will be using the w3 validator constantly to make sure all my work is correct. Also, I installed the HTML Linter Package which tells me if and where I have errors.

In the fourteenth week, we were introduced to addressing in the web where the lecturer explained bit by bit where and what every part of the web address means. To begin with, we looked at IP Addresses and found out that binary addresses are converted into the web addresses that we see. From there, depending on the page you are on, you will find the name of the domain and after the backslash you will find all the code such as the video which would be in example, video.php. In my own time i studied this a bit further on different pages of the same website. I could find certain parts that were included in that webpage, just by looking at the address.

In the fifteenth week, we looked at usability of the website, within this lecture and practical we looked at what is appropriate and what is not. You can find certain web pages which are unhelpful, such as a form on a website with no submit button. Personally that and made me think what i need to do in my own website which makes the usability effectively and correctly accordingly to the page on my website for my coursework. This proved to be an easy task for me as there was not much to do on my website apart from view. My job was to insure that everything is usable to every user.

In the sixteenth week, we discussed accessibility of a website for those who are less able than the average human. To compliment this website we had a practical task where we had ice cream sticks and latex disposable gloves. We were assigned with tasks to do using the gloves and also glassed that meant that your vision was impaired. This taught me that i should go for a very simple and user friendly design and simplistic use over the over complex features for my coursework and you have to take into account the users that will be using the website.

In the seventeenth week, we looked at the SVG programming language in both lectures and practicals. With this we can create our own images which can be exported and saved in the format of picture types such as .jpeg and .png. In our practical, we were challenged by our lecturer, Dr. Rich Boakes, to a drawing challenge of the university logo and beat him to it. I lost to him as he has experience. However, i did learn a lot about how to draw the university logo just using shapes. One thing i did to practice, was use graph paper to draw it out with its size in cm, to work out how to make it using maths and i found that to be very help.

In the eighteenth week, we looked at javascript. We looked at making stickmans and making them move around. The good thing i found about this is that i was able to get an insight of the second year, the one thing i found great about this is the animated version on the web that is interactive. We were also shown how to make a game using stickmans with diemans. I personally used this when looking into creating a slider for a website, so when you click a button it will scroll down automatically.
